ⓘ Map guide

Every operating natural-gas power plant in PJM (199 plants, 94 GW total) is screened for one question: is there enough developable land within 10 km to build a new data center plus enough new solar to serve a flat 24/7 load equal to the plant's capacity, with the gas plant only covering 5% / 10% / 20% backup?

A first-pass land-availability screen — not a cost, dispatch, or interconnection study.

How to use it

  • Search (top) for a place name or lat, lon to drop a pin and zoom there.
  • Click any plant to draw its 10 km circle and shade the land that could host solar.
  • Use the Street / Satellite switch (top-right) to see what's actually on that land.
  • Drag the Nameplate filter sliders (top-left) to show only plants in a MW range (e.g. min 1000 to isolate the ≥ 1 GW plants) — green and red both; reset clears it.
  • Hover/click a marker for its popup with the numbers below.

Map symbols

green marker
Plant qualifies — enough developable land at the 10% gas-cap case.
red marker
Does not qualify — solar+DC land won't fit within 10 km (usually a very large plant).
 –  marker size
Proportional to the plant's nameplate capacity (MW) = the size of the data-center load being tested.
green hatch
Developable solar land within 10 km at full 30 m detail. Gaps (holes) are land removed by the screen — water, wetlands, developed areas, steep slope, forest, protected areas.
◯ dashed circle
The 10 km buffer — solar/DC land is only counted inside it.

Terms in the popup

Nameplate (MW)
Plant's max rated output; here it doubles as the data-center load size.
AC capacity factor
Solar output ÷ its theoretical max, from PVWatts/NSRDB. PJM range here 0.17–0.21. Higher = less solar needed.
Developable (MW / km²)
Buildable solar land in the buffer × power density (~35 MW/km²) = how much solar physically fits.
Headroom
Developable solar ÷ solar required. ≥ 1.0 qualifies; shown for each gas cap. Higher = more slack.
Gas cap (g)
Share of annual data-center energy the gas plant may supply (5% / 10% / 20%). Lower g = cleaner but needs more solar/land.

The test (per plant, per gas cap)

solar needed = 1.3 × (1 − g) / CF × nameplate  ·   land needed = solar ÷ 35 MW/km² (+ 150-acre data-center parcel). Qualifies when developable land ≥ land needed.

At a glance

91 of 199 plants qualify at the 10% gas cap (conservative, forest-excluded case), hosting 18 GW of clean-ish data-center load.

Read with care

  • The hatch shows the conservative screen (all forest excluded). Including forest roughly doubles the land — a documented sensitivity, not shown here.
  • Land being present ≠ water/fiber/grid capacity being available. This screens land only.
  • Capacity factor here is mildly optimistic; results are a plausibility screen, not a guarantee.
