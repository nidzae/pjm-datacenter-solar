ⓘ Map guide

Every operating natural-gas power plant in PJM (199 plants, 94 GW total) is screened for one question: is there enough developable land within 10 km to build a new data center plus enough new solar to serve a flat 24/7 load equal to the plant's capacity, with the gas plant only covering 5% / 10% / 20% backup?

A first-pass land-availability screen — not a cost, dispatch, or interconnection study.

How to use it

  • Search (top) for a place name or lat, lon to drop a pin and zoom there.
  • Click any plant to draw its 10 km circle and shade the land that could host solar.
  • Use the Street / Satellite switch (top-right) to see what's actually on that land.
  • Drag the Hostable-load filter sliders (top-left) to show only plants that can host a data-center of a given size (MW). This uses each plant's solar-limited hostable load (the partial capacity that fits), not full nameplate; reset clears it.
  • Hover/click a marker for its popup with the numbers below.

Terms in the popup

Key point: a plant qualifies when the solar that fits ≥ the solar needed — not when developable MW > nameplate MW. A flat 24/7 load needs roughly 6× its size in solar panels (panels run only ~19% of the time, plus overbuild for storage losses and cloudy stretches), so a 1 GW load can need ~6 GW of solar.

Nameplate (MW)
Plant's max rated output; here it's the size of the 24/7 data-center load.
AC capacity factor
Solar output ÷ its theoretical max, from PVWatts/NSRDB. PJM range here 0.17–0.21. Higher = less solar needed.
Developable land / usable for solar
Developable land × power density (~91 MW/mi²) = the solar the land could fit. But a fixed 150-acre parcel is reserved for the data center building, so usable for solar = (land − 150 ac) × power density. On small urban sites this deduction can matter; on normal sites the solar dwarfs it.
Solar needed
= 1.3 × (1 − g) / CF × nameplate. The panels required so annual solar energy covers the load (minus the gas share). ~6× nameplate at these capacity factors.
Hostable DC load (solar-limited)
The data-center size the usable solar land can actually power = min(nameplate, usable solar ÷ 1.3(1−g)/CF). Full nameplate for green plants; the partial data center you could still build for red ones. The hostable-load slider filters on this.
Headroom
Usable solar (after the 150-ac DC parcel) ÷ solar needed. ≥ 1.0 = full nameplate qualifies, exactly; shown per gas cap.
Gas cap (g)
Share of annual data-center energy the gas plant may supply (5% / 10% / 20%). Lower g = cleaner but needs more solar/land.

The test (per plant, per gas cap)

solar needed = 1.3 × (1 − g) / CF × nameplate  ·   land needed = solar ÷ 91 MW/mi² (+ 150-acre data-center parcel). Qualifies when developable land ≥ land needed.

At a glance

91 of 199 plants fit a full-nameplate data center at the 10% gas cap (conservative, forest-excluded) = 18 GW. But counting the partial data centers that the other sites can still host, total solar-limited hostable load rises to ~44 GW — the opportunity beyond the whole-plant winners.

Read with care

  • The hatch shows the conservative screen (all forest excluded). Including forest roughly doubles the land — a documented sensitivity, not shown here.
  • Land being present ≠ water/fiber/grid capacity being available. This screens land only.
  • Capacity factor here is mildly optimistic; results are a plausibility screen, not a guarantee.
